Image Sets

About | 05 | A | B | Create

I make web-based tools that generate images. Currently, I am working on a tool that:

  1. Allows me to record a doodle.
  2. The drawing automatically becomes a background layer.
  3. Random sections of the drawing are automatically redrawn and added to the image.
  4. Based on a random selection of color functions, parts of the drawing are emphasized or augmented.

I'm programmatically doodling over a doodle of a doodle.

The extent of the randomness can be refined with settings that give me more or less control. I can set things up so new images are constantly generated. I see them arriving and delete them if they donâ€™t seem to work.

In practice, what I do is strangely reminiscent of making monoprints because it's not clear quite what the image will look like until peeling back the paper.

Recently, I generated an image with something at the bottom of the page. I am struggling to know why I am drawn to it and what it's saying to me. I like working in this kind of space. Sometimes it's like someone else made the image.

Collaboration: I'd like other people to be able to create their own gallery on my website using the tool I outline above. It could get complicated, but the main thing would be that they would provide a drawing on screen then pick settings and make sections as images are generated.

I find I tinker with the code rather than use it to create. I think it's time to stop coding and focus on using the tool I have built to create substantial sets of images. I think it's reached that point of maturity. I am also interested in getting back to making physical artwork, probably with the plotter, maybe with paint.
