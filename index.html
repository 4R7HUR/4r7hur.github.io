Digital Monoprinting

I make web-based tools that generate images. Currently, I am working on a tool that:

  1. Allows me to record a line drawing.
  2. Plucks random sections of the drawing and redraws them.
  3. Makes a random selection of colour functions, these are used to re-work areas of the drawing.

Randomness: The extent of the randomness can be refined with settings that give me more control. There are lots of settings, it becomes an itterative process of refing the possible range of outcomes. I can set things up so new images are constantly generated, I see them arriving and delete them if they donâ€™t seem to work.

Digital Monoprinting: In practice, what I do is reminds be a lot of making monoprints because it's not clear quite what the image will look like until peeling back the paper. I struggle to understand why some of my generated images appear to be more successful, but I like working in this kind of space and sometimes it feels like someone else made the image.

Collaboration: I'd like other people to be able to create their own gallery on my website using my tools. It could get complicated, but the main thing would be that they would provide a drawing on screen then pick settings and make selections as images are generated. In the past I have tried creating programs that generate images without a drawing as a starting place, however at the moment I am more interested in feeding a drawing in at the start.

Physical Images: It would be interesting to get back to making some physical artwork, that said I want to be careful not to pull focus from my current process which seems to be important to what I am doing.

Slowing things down: I'm interested in slowing the time it takes for my code to run so I can record and then watch the activity.
